Car Seats

In the United States, children within a specified weight and height must ride in a car seat. There are different types of car seats and some can be used in various stages of a childs' development.

Car Seat types are:

  • Rear-Facing Car Seat
  • Forward-Facing Car Seat
  • Booster seat
  • Seat Belt

A Rear-Facing Car Seat is intended for newborns up to about age one, depending on the childs' weight and height. Since infant car seats are produced by various companies, the manual that accompanies the car seat will need to be checked for height and weight recommendations. Infant car seats are three types. To learn more visit the Rear-Facing Car Seats page.

Forward-facing Car Seats faces the front of the vehicle or in the position that places the child facing the front of the vehicle. These types of car seats are intended for children, given they have met the weight height recommendations indicated by the manufacturer, who are mature beyond the infant stage. Booster Seats are another type of seat and is more common used among elmentary-age children. Boosters are of four types, indicated here.

The use of Seat Belts is the final stage and children typically begin using seat belts during their older elmentary years through adulthood.
